2. What Data We Collect

We may collect and process the following personal data:

  • Contact enquiries: Name, email address, company name, service enquiry and message content
  • Job applications: Full name, email address, role applied for, GitHub profile URL, LinkedIn profile URL and personal statement
  • Usage data: IP address, browser type, pages visited, time on site and referring URL (via Google Analytics)
  • Cookie data: See our Cookie Policy for full details

We do not collect sensitive personal data (such as racial or ethnic origin, health data, or financial information) through our website.

3. How We Collect Your Data

We collect data through the following means:

  • Contact form on our Contact page — when you send an enquiry
  • Application form on our Join Us page — when you apply for a role
  • Google Analytics — automatically when you browse our website (with your consent)
  • Email correspondence — when you contact us directly by email

5. How We Store Your Data

Your data is processed through Formspree (our form submission service) and delivered to our secure business email hosted at Zoho Mail. Form submissions are not stored on our website servers.

Website analytics are processed by Google Analytics, subject to Google's own privacy policies and your cookie consent choices.

We take appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure or access.

6. Who We Share Your Data With

We do not sell, rent or trade your personal data to third parties.

We may share data with the following trusted third-party service providers who process data on our behalf:

  • Formspree — form submission processing (USA, Standard Contractual Clauses)
  • Google Analytics — website analytics (USA, Standard Contractual Clauses)
  • Zoho Mail — email hosting (EU/UK data centres)
  • GitHub — website hosting via GitHub Pages

We may also disclose your data where required by law, court order or regulatory authority.

7. International Transfers

Some of our third-party service providers are based outside the UK. Where we transfer personal data outside the UK, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

  • UK adequacy regulations
  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the ICO
  • The provider's certification under an approved framework

9. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, you have the following rights:

  • Right of access — Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to rectification — Ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure — Ask us to delete your personal data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to restrict processing — Ask us to limit how we use your data
  • Right to data portability — Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to object — Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to withdraw consent — Withdraw consent at any time where processing is based on consent

To exercise any of these rights, email us at info@balancewises.io with the subject line "Data Rights Request". We will respond within 30 days.

If you are unhappy with how we handle your data, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO): ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint  ·  0303 123 1113
